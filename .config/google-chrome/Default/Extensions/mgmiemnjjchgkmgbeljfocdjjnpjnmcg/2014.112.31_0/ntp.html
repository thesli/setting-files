Awesome New Tab Page's development is supported by optional injected ads.

Awesome New Tab Page's development is supported by optional injected advertisements that are added to some of the websites you visit. Over the past 2+ years, I've put in thousands of hours developing this extension (adding features, fixing bugs, making things better), supporting all users who ask for help (yep, all!), and developing (and helping other widget developers develop) widgets. My goal, as it always has been, is to make awesome stuff for awesome people.

Ads support most of the internet we all use and love; without them, the internet we have today would simply not exist. Similarly, without revenue, this extension (and the upcoming Awesome HQ, which is in beta) would not be possible.

You can disable these injected ads in the settings (click the cog wheel in the top left of the page). If you disable the ads in settings, they should remain disabled unless you reinstall or reset the extension.


Types of ads
Superfish Similar Products

Superfish ads are deals and similar products. You can use it to quickly search other websites for the same or similar products. It's generally accessed by briefly mousing over a button that appears on product images.

RevJet Coupons

RevJet ads are deals and coupons added to select websites that can help you save money.

The ads used in Awesome New Tab Page adhere to the Chrome Web Store's ad policies that can be found here. If you have any questions, please don't hesitate to contact me here (Contact support link on the right).

I want to make things very clear how the injected ads work and remind you that, as always with Awesome New Tab Page, you have complete control over pretty much everything. Thanks for your understanding and (maybe) support.
